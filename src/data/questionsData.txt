COMMUNICATION STYLE QUIZ
Questions and Answers

Style Key:
  A = Direct        (speed, clarity, decisive action)
  B = Enthusiastic  (energy, motivation, positive vibe)
  C = Collaborative (care, connection, shared problem-solving)
  D = Precise       (accuracy, standards, detail)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 1.
A team member used the wrong milk in a smoothie. What sounds most like you?

  A) "Wrong base. Remake it with almond milk and keep the line moving."
  B) "No worries! Let's knock out a perfect remake and get it to them with a smile."
  C) "Rough one, I've been there. What can I do to help you get the right one out?"
  D) "Double check the ticket — they swapped almond milk. Read every line carefully for the remake."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 2.
A team member isn't offering enhancers. How do you coach them?

  A) "Use this line: 'Would you like to add an energy boost or extra protein?' Try it for the next ten guests."
  B) "You're great with guests! Now, tell them why you love adding a multivitamin to your own smoothie."
  C) "It feels a bit awkward to ask, doesn't it? Let's find a way to say it that feels like you."
  D) "If we add an enhancer to 10% more orders, our store hits its goal."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 3.
It's shift change. How do you brief the incoming lead?

  A) "Lobby is clean and prep is caught up. John comes in at 6. You're set."
  B) "Team is in a great groove! Awesome energy at the window — just keep that momentum going."
  C) "Team's good, but Sarah had a tough rush — check on her. Anything you need from me?"
  D) "Strawberry inventory is low and the walk-in is organized by date. Waste is logged in the book."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 4.
A new seasonal smoothie starts today. What sounds most like you?

  A) "New smoothie today. Watch your build order and keep tickets under three minutes."
  B) "This flavor is a game-changer! Taste it so you can get the guests hyped about it."
  C) "We have a new launch. I want to hear what you guys think of the flavor profile."
  D) "This one has more ingredients than some of our smoothies, so follow the ticket exactly."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 5.
A team member is fast but isn't greeting guests warmly. What's your move?

  A) "Your speed is great, but you need to greet guests as soon as they walk in."
  B) "You're a machine on blenders! Now, let's bring that same energy to the front door."
  C) "I love how reliable you are. How can we make the greeting feel more natural for you?"
  D) "Surveys show the greeting is our most important metric. Let's make sure we check that box."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 6.
A team member who is usually on time arrives 15 minutes late. What sounds most like you?

  A) "You're late. Clock in and get on blenders — we'll talk about the schedule later."
  B) "Glad you made it! We need your energy on the line — grab your apron and jump in."
  C) "Glad you're safe. Is everything okay? Take a second to breathe, then join us."
  D) "You're 15 minutes behind. Note the correct time on the log before you wash up."
