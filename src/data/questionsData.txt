COMMUNICATION STYLE QUIZ
Questions and Answers

Style Key:
  A = Direct        (speed, clarity, decisive action)
  B = Enthusiastic  (energy, motivation, positive vibe)
  C = Collaborative (care, connection, shared problem-solving)
  D = Precise       (accuracy, standards, detail)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 1.
A team member makes a mistake on a custom smoothie during a busy rush. What is your first instinct?

  A) "Let's remake it quickly so we can keep the line moving — I'll let the guest know."
  B) "No worries, it happens. Let's get a fresh one going."
  C) "Take a breath — want me to jump in and finish this one with you?"
  D) "Let's take a quick look at the recipe card together so we can spot where the ratio went off."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 2.
You are training a brand-new hire on the register. What do you focus on most during their first hour?

  A) Showing them the quickest way to ring in orders and modifiers so the line keeps moving.
  B) Keeping the energy up so they feel comfortable and engaged with guests.
  C) Checking in often to make sure they feel supported and aren't stressed by the screen.
  D) Walking them through our cash handling steps and how to correctly enter modifier requests.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 3.
It is time to hand off the shift to the incoming manager. How do you communicate the store's status?

  A) I give a clear, bulleted rundown of what is done and what still needs to happen.
  B) I share a few wins from the day and set a positive tone for their shift.
  C) I ask how they are doing and see if there is anything I can wrap up to set them up well before I leave.
  D) I walk them through current inventory, waste, and prep levels in the walk-in.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 4.
A new seasonal smoothie is launching today. How do you introduce it to your team?

  A) I cover the build order, prep time, and how it may affect our speed of service.
  B) I talk up the flavor, why guests will enjoy it, and get the team excited to recommend it.
  C) I have everyone taste it, ask for their thoughts, and make sure they feel confident making it.
  D) I review the nutritional info, measurement changes, and allergen notes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 5.
A guest looks overwhelmed by the menu and asks for a recommendation. How do you reply?

  A) I ask what they're looking for (protein, vegan, low-calorie) and suggest our top seller in that category.
  B) I share a personal favorite and tell them what I like about it.
  C) I ask a couple of questions about what they're in the mood for and help guide them toward something that fits.
  D) I ask about their flavor and ingredient preferences and walk them through a couple of options.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 6.
You notice the lobby is getting messy, but there is a moderate line at the register. What do you say?

  A) "John, the line's manageable — can you head out and sweep the lobby?"
  B) "Hey team, let's take a couple of minutes to tidy up the lobby before the next rush."
  C) "Does anyone have a moment to grab the lobby, or should we all stay on the line for now?"
  D) "The lobby checklist is due — wipe down the tables first, then check the trash bins."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 7.
A team member arrives 15 minutes late for their shift. How do you greet them?

  A) "Go ahead and clock in — once you're ready, jump on the blender station, tickets are backing up."
  B) "Glad you're here — grab your apron and let's jump in."
  C) "Everything okay? Let's get you settled in once you're ready."
  D) "Please note your arrival time in the logbook, then wash up before stepping on the floor."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 8.
The blender rinser breaks down in the middle of a busy shift. How do you communicate the plan?

  A) I quickly explain the new workflow for rinsing pitchers by hand so ticket times don't slip.
  B) I let the team know we'll be doing it the old-school way today and keep the mood light while we work through it.
  C) I check in with the person on blender to make sure they're not getting overwhelmed by the bottleneck.
  D) I pull the maintenance manual to troubleshoot and log a detailed ticket in the system.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 9.
During your pre-shift huddle, what is your primary talking point?

  A) Hitting today's sales targets and keeping our labor on track.
  B) Setting a good tone for the shift — music on, energy up, and a positive atmosphere.
  C) Making sure everyone is comfortable with their station and feels supported by the team.
  D) Reviewing our core standards, like portion control and avoiding cross-contamination.

Question 10.
You need to give feedback to a team member who is struggling to offer add-ins (upselling). How do you approach it?

  A) I give them a short, simple script they can use with every guest to keep it consistent.
  B) I encourage them, point out what they already do well at the register, and acknowledge it the next time they land an add-in.
  C) I ask how they feel about upselling and try to understand what makes it uncomfortable for them.
  D) I show them the numbers on how one extra add-in per transaction affects store metrics and their own performance.
